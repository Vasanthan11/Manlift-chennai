Where scissor lifts are most useful

Stable platforms for repeated up-and-down work.

Scissor lifts are designed for jobs where your team needs to stand on a level platform, move equipment with them and travel up and down multiple times in a day.

When your work is mostly vertical – directly above the machine – scissor lifts provide a simple and efficient way to reach height. The platform rails give technicians space to move around with tools, fittings and materials.

In many sites they replace temporary ladders and basic staging, especially where tasks repeat over several days. The ability to drive the machine at low height also speeds up how crews move between work zones.

Indoor scissor lifts

Electric scissor lifts for finished floors and controlled spaces.

Electric scissor lifts are preferred in buildings where noise and emissions must be kept low – such as IT parks, commercial interiors and operational warehouses.

These lifts are designed to work on finished concrete floors, tiles and other prepared surfaces. Their compact size allows movement through doors, loading areas and internal aisles while keeping turning radius manageable.

Non-marking tyres can be used in many indoor environments to limit visible tyre marks. Battery operation also reduces exhaust fumes and sound levels inside the building.

If you share your floor type, available aisle width and height requirement, we can propose electric scissor lift options that match both the work and the building’s constraints.

Outdoor & rough-terrain scissors

Platforms for external work and developing ground.

Some projects need scissor lifts that can handle unfinished surfaces outside the building – for example, early-phase construction or external services work.

Rough-terrain scissor lifts are built with larger tyres and stronger frames to cope with uneven, partially prepared ground. They are commonly used for façade work, external service installation and temporary access around plant structures.

These machines often have higher platform capacities, allowing teams to carry extra materials and fittings to the work zone. They are typically diesel-powered and suited to open-air environments.

Share photos of the approach road, work area and expected working height. We can review whether rough-terrain scissor lifts will be suitable or if another access solution is safer.
